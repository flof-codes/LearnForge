# LearnForge Brand Identity

## The Golden Circle

### Why (Purpose)
I kept forgetting things I'd already learned. Every flashcard app showed me the same card, the same way, and I started recognizing the image instead of recalling the concept. I built LearnForge because I believe the science of memory deserves better tools -- and nobody should have to choose between AI-powered learning and actually retaining knowledge long-term.

### How (Method)
LearnForge combines spaced repetition science (FSRS) with Bloom's Taxonomy cognitive depth tracking and AI-powered dynamic question generation. Every review is different. The system knows what you know, how deeply you know it, and asks exactly the right question to make sure you never forget.

### What (Product)
An open-source learning platform with an MCP server that turns AI conversations into scientifically scheduled flashcards -- with dynamic reformulation, cognitive depth progression, and multiple study modalities.

## Brand Archetype: The Memory Guardian

LearnForge is not a teacher. It doesn't help you acquire new knowledge -- that's what books, lectures, and AI conversations do. LearnForge is **the guardian of what you already know**. It sits between you and the forgetting curve, knowing exactly where your knowledge gaps are, and asking precisely the right questions to make sure nothing slips away.

**Primary Archetype: The Sage** (focused on retention and understanding depth)
- Core desire: Ensure no knowledge is ever lost
- Strategy: Track cognitive depth per concept, adapt questions accordingly
- Messaging: "We know what you know -- and what you're about to forget"

**Secondary Archetype: The Creator** (user builds their own mastery)
- The learner isn't passive -- they actively construct understanding through progressively harder questions
- From recall to analysis to creation: every concept is a journey, not a checkbox

## Core Messaging

### The Hook
**"Never see the same question twice."**

This is the single most visceral, easy-to-grasp differentiator. Every learner who has used flashcards knows the feeling of recognizing a card's layout instead of recalling the concept. This line immediately signals: "We solved that."

### The Elevator Pitch (one paragraph)
Traditional flashcard apps show you the same card, the same way, every time. You end up memorizing what the card looks like instead of understanding the concept. LearnForge changes that -- every review is dynamically reformulated by AI, and the system tracks how deeply you understand each concept using Bloom's Taxonomy. Basic recall today, critical analysis next week. Combined with FSRS spaced repetition and multiple study modalities, it's the learning tool that actually knows what you know.

### Key Messages (priority order)

1. **Dynamic reformulation** -- Every review phrases the question differently. You can't fake understanding by recognizing a card layout.
2. **Cognitive depth tracking** -- Bloom's Taxonomy progression from Remember to Create. The system knows whether you can recall a fact or truly analyze a concept.
3. **AI-native learning** -- Create cards through conversation. Study via multiple-choice, open questions, or classic self-rating. Your AI tutor remembers everything.
4. **Science-backed scheduling** -- FSRS algorithm with modality-aware intervals. Harder study modalities earn longer intervals.
5. **Open source** -- Self-hostable, transparent, community-driven. Your learning data stays yours.

### Competition Positioning
**Reference the problem, don't name competitors.**

Instead of "Unlike Anki...", say "Traditional flashcard apps..." or "Most spaced repetition tools..."

The problems to reference:
- Static cards that never change (the recognition trap)
- Manual card creation (tedious, time-consuming)
- Flat difficulty (always the same level of questioning)
- Self-rating bias (learners overestimate their understanding)
- Single modality (always the same Q&A format)

## Bloom's Taxonomy -- How We Explain It

Bloom's Taxonomy is LearnForge's deepest differentiator but also the hardest to explain simply. Here's how to communicate it at different levels of detail:

### One-liner (for hero/features)
"Tracks how deeply you understand each concept -- from basic recall to critical thinking."

### Short explanation (for feature section)
Most flashcard apps only test one thing: can you remember the answer? LearnForge goes deeper. It tracks six levels of understanding for every concept:

| Level | What it means | Example question |
|-------|--------------|------------------|
| Remember | Can you recall the fact? | "What is osmosis?" |
| Understand | Can you explain it? | "Why does a cell shrink in salt water?" |
| Apply | Can you use it? | "Calculate the osmotic pressure of this solution." |
| Analyze | Can you break it down? | "Compare osmosis and active transport." |
| Evaluate | Can you judge it? | "Is this researcher's claim about osmosis valid?" |
| Create | Can you build with it? | "Design an experiment to measure osmosis rate." |

As you prove mastery, LearnForge automatically escalates the difficulty. You graduate from "what is it?" to "what would you do with it?"

### Simplified tagline
"From 'what is it?' to 'what would you do with it?'"

## Target Audience

### Primary: Students in demanding fields
University students (medicine, law, STEM, languages) who need serious retention tools. They already know spaced repetition works but are frustrated by existing tools.

### Secondary: Lifelong learners
Curious adults learning professional certifications, new skills, languages. They learn because they want to, not because they have to.

## Community & Feedback Framing

### Primary angle: "Shape the future"
Users are co-creators, not consumers. Their feedback directly shapes the roadmap.

- "Every feature request gets read."
- "This is v1 -- and your voice decides what v2 looks like."
- Public roadmap (GitHub Projects or changelog on landing page)

### Secondary angle: "Join early adopters"
There's an energy to getting in early. Frame it as opportunity, not risk.

- "You're getting in early. Help build the learning tool that should have existed years ago."
- Show momentum signals: recent commits, star count, version number

### Framing the early stage
Never apologize for being new. Frame it as:
- **Momentum**: "Actively developed -- new features every week"
- **Opportunity**: "Shape the roadmap while it's still being drawn"
- **Honesty**: "v1 with a clear vision for where this is going"

### Support / Donations
Lead with value, not need:
- "Love LearnForge? Help keep it independent."
- "Fuel open-source learning science."
- Never guilt-driven. Always optional. Frame subscription as the primary support mechanism.

## Visual Identity Principles

### Theme
The app supports light, dark, and auto themes. The landing page should respect the visitor's system preference (auto), with a toggle available.

### Emotional Direction
- **Clean, not sterile** -- Generous whitespace, clear hierarchy, but with warmth
- **Precise, not cold** -- Technical quality with friendly touches (subtle gradients, warm accents)
- **Modern, not trendy** -- Timeless dark/light palette, no gimmicks that age quickly
- **Atmospheric** -- Subtle glows and gradients that create depth without distraction

### Color Principles
- Dark mode: Deep backgrounds (#111827), soft text (#e0e4ef), atmospheric accent glows
- Light mode: Clean whites, sharp contrast, same accent palette
- Accent colors should reflect the Bloom levels -- a progression from green (Remember) through cyan, purple, to warm tones (Create)
- The Bloom color spectrum doubles as the brand's signature visual element

### Typography Principles
- Clean, readable sans-serif (current: system/Tailwind defaults)
- Bold headlines with tight tracking for impact
- Generous line-height for body text (readability over density)
- Monospace for code/technical elements only

### Iconography
- Outline style, consistent stroke weight
- Lucide icons (already used in the app)
- Simple, functional -- not decorative
